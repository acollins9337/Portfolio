01

ROS2 Autonomous Maze Solver

I spent some time working with a partner on a software package to solve a simple maze. The maze was constructed such that signs on the walls pointed the way to the goal, and were key to solving the maze. Aditya and I created a ROS2/Python package to solve the maze by using machine learning to predict what the signs are, and then traveling to waypoints using ROS2 NAV2. Our final submission achieved 90% sign classification accuracy, and was able to complete the maze properly and without error in 5/6 tests.

03

VEX Compatible Planetary Wheels

I started this project with the goal of innovating on conventional VEX Robotics drivetrains by improving the space efficiency of the systems. My goal was to design a wheel that could fit a planetary gear train inside of it to enable competitive drivetrain speeds while eliminating the need for long gear trains. The final product I designed uses a custom set of gears designed in Autodesk Inventor to enable a full planetary gear assembly to fit around a standard VEX shaft. The wheel design is designed to interface with standard VEX Omni-wheel components for ease of use and to cut down on manufacturing and assembly time. This project forced me to balance multiple aspects of design, including manufacturing time, efficiency, and practicality.
